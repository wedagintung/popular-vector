Lysergic acid is made by alkaline hydrolysis of lysergamides like ergotamine a substance. LSD has two labile protons at the tertiary stereogenic C5 and C8 positions rendering these centres prone to epimerisation. The lysergic acid is treated with other chemicals and heat to rearrange its chemical structure. Its basic chemical structure is similar to that of the ergot alkaloids and it is structurally related to several other drugs eg bufotenine psilocybin harmine and ibogaine which can all block the action of serotonin the indole amine transmitter of nerve impulses in brain tissue.
